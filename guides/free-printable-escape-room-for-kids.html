🔐 The Free Mini Escape Room: 'The Great Party Vault'

Here is a complete clue trail you can run today. Three clues are hidden around one or two rooms; each one hides a single secret number and points to where the next clue is waiting. Collect all three numbers — 5, then 2, then 4 — and the vault opens. It takes most kids 10 to 20 minutes, and the youngest players need no reading skills at all if a grown-up reads each clue aloud.

Setup takes about five minutes: hide the treat (party bags, the cake, a sticker sheet, or a 'You Escaped!' note) inside your box and lock or tape it shut; write or print the three clues below onto separate cards; tape Clue 2 to the fridge and tuck Clue 3 in the bathroom; and keep Clue 1 in your pocket to hand out. Then gather everyone, put on your best mystery-narrator voice, and announce that the party treasure is locked in the vault.

When they bring you 5, 2, and 4, it is showtime: dial 5-2-4 (or dramatically 'unlock' the taped box) and let them fling it open. Cue the cheering, the treats, and — if you fancy a keepsake — a quick hand-written 'You Escaped!' certificate with each child's name on it.

  1. Clue 1 — The Counting Clue (hand this out to begin): 'Welcome, agents! Count the stars: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐. How many are there? That is your FIRST number. Now creep to where the cold milk lives.' Answer: 5 → sends them to the fridge.
  2. Clue 2 — The Sorting Clue (taped to the fridge): 'Sort the fruit! There are apples 🍎🍎 and blueberries 🫐🫐🫐. How many APPLES? That is your SECOND number. Now tiptoe to where you brush your teeth.' Answer: 2 → off to the bathroom.
  3. Clue 3 — The Spotter's Clue (waiting in the bathroom): 'Eyes sharp! ⚡⚡⚡⚡ How many lightning bolts can you count? That is your THIRD number. Race back to the VAULT with all three!' Answer: 4 → the code is complete.

🎈 How to Run It So Everyone Has Fun

A few small touches turn three clue cards into a proper adventure. None of these are essential, but they are the difference between 'that was nice' and 'again, again!'

  • Commit to the story. A silly accent and a gentle countdown ('the vault re-locks in 20 minutes!') raise the stakes without any real pressure.
  • Play in teams for mixed ages — pair an older 'captain' who reads the clues with younger 'code-breakers' who do the counting.
  • Using a real padlock? The numbers go in order — 5, then 2, then 4 — so keep the clue sequence clear. A taped box doesn't mind the order.
  • Write or print the clues in colour if you can; counting and spotting are far easier when the items pop off the page.
  • Keep the timer loose for under-sixes. The goal is giggles, not a stopwatch meltdown.
  • End big: a high-five, a certificate, and the treat waiting inside the vault.

👶 Adjust It for Ages 4 to 9

The same trail flexes across the whole 4-to-9 range — you just dial the difficulty up or down.

For ages 4-5: read every clue aloud, keep the counts under six, and hide all the clues in one room so there is no wandering. Two clues instead of three is perfectly fine.

For ages 6-7: let them read the clues themselves, spread the hiding spots across two rooms, and add a fourth clue — a simple 'which path isn't blocked?' maze gives you a fourth digit.

For ages 8-9: add a mini cipher. Write A=1, B=2, C=3 on a card and have them decode a short word into numbers. That is exactly the kind of step our themed kits use to keep older kids challenged.

🧩 When Kids Get Stuck (Gentle Hints)

Little solvers hit walls, and a stuck child is a sad child — so keep a soft hint ready instead of the answer. The trick is to nudge the method, not hand over the number.

  • Counting clue: 'Touch each star as you say the number out loud.'
  • Sorting clue: 'Put all the apples in one pile first — now how big is the pile?'
  • Spotting clue: 'Point to each bolt and give it a number as you go.'
  • Totally stuck? Whisper the number and let them 'discover' it. A finished game always beats a perfect one.

Puzzle 1: ⭐ Clue 1 — Count the Stars

This card shows a busy night sky crammed with shapes — twinkly stars, curvy moons and little clouds all jumbled together. The mission: count ONLY the gold stars, and ignore every moon and cloud (they're there to trick you!). Top tip for younger agents: touch each star and circle it with your pen as you count, so none get counted twice and none get missed. The total number of stars is the FIRST number of the vault code — write it in the star box at the bottom of the card so you don't forget it.

You need: Just the printed Clue 1 card and a pen or crayon.
Answer: There are exactly 4 gold stars hidden among the moons and clouds. First digit = 4. → write down the digit 4

This card has a doorway on the left and three treasure chests down the right-hand side, with muddy footprints winding all over the page. Start at the doorway and trace the real, connected trail with your finger or pen, footprint by footprint. (The dead-end chests have decoy numbers on them, so follow the footsteps carefully and don't get tricked!)
